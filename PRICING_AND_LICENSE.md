Purpose
Provide pricing tiers and licensing recommendations for distributing and supporting VUA TOTALITY.

Suggested Licensing Options
1) Open-source with paid services (recommended for adoption)
- License: MIT or BSD-3-Clause for code permissiveness.
- Offer paid support, hardened builds, and commercial licenses for enterprises.

2) Dual-license model
- Offer code under an open-source license (AGPL or MIT) and commercial license for closed-source internal use.
- Useful if you want to retain stronger commercial control.

3) Proprietary license
- Keep project closed-source and sell licenses. Higher revenue but slower adoption.

Recommended: MIT + Paid Support / Commercial License Offer
- MIT encourages adoption.
- Provide `commercial-support` agreements and paid add-ons (SLA, managed hosting, attestation archival).

Pricing Tiers (example)

Tier | Description | One-time | Monthly
---|---:|---:|---:
Hobby / Self-host | Code only, community support | $0 | $0
Professional | Includes setup & 1 month support | $750 | $50/month (hosting minimal)
Business | Onboarding, 3 months support, extra features | $3,000 | $300/month (monitoring + backups)
Enterprise (SLA) | Dedicated onboarding, custom integrations, SLA | $10,000+ | $1,500+/month

Add-ons
- Longer-term attestation archival (per GB/month)
- Custom integrations & automation (time & materials)
- White-glove security review (fixed-fee)

Discounts & Licensing Notes
- Offer discounts for educational / non-profit use.
- Provide fixed-fee yearly license for enterprises needing on-prem distribution.
- Always provide clear attribution terms in commercial agreements if required.

Billing cadence and contract length
- Monthly subscription for hosting/support, invoiced monthly.
- One-time professional services invoiced on completion or 50% upfront / 50% on delivery.

Legal note
- Choose a permissive OSS license (MIT/BSD) and add a `COMMERCIAL_LICENSE.md` outlining terms for paid customers. Consult counsel for binding contracts.
